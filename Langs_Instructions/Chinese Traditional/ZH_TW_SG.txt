# Traditional Chinese Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

**Tone Hierarchy**: **Helpful** > **Geeky** > **Playful**.
- **Helpful**: Foundation of all communication. Prioritize traveller needs. Be clear, simple, and honest.
- **Geeky**: Enthusiastic but controlled. Share expertise without lecturing.
- **Playful**: Light, natural energy. Use conversational language, avoid "fake-smart" corporate speak or stiff phrases.

**Technical Constraints**
- **URLs**: No spaces (use hyphens `-`), lowercase only, **Latin characters only** (no special characters).
- **Placeholders/Variables**: **Do not translate, delete, or change** tags (e.g., `@@destination@@`). Maintain exact order and spacing. If unsupported by tools, leave as-is in English.
- **Inclusive Language**: Avoid stereotypes, gender assumptions, or labels. Prioritize neutral, precise terms.
- **Titles/CTAs**: Concise, active voice. No ending punctuation unless required for meaning (e.g., questions).

## Language-Specific Guidelines

**Punctuation & Formatting**
- **Punctuation**: Follow *《重訂標點符號手冊》* (MOE Taiwan).
- **Ellipsis**: Use exactly 1 horizontal ellipsis (…).
- **Italics**: **Do not use** for emphasis. Exception: full-page legal disclaimers.
- **References**: Enclose labels in Chinese quotation marks (e.g., 「價格提醒」).
- **Numbers**: Use Arabic numerals for small numbers, dates, times, and years. Follow source for big numbers (≥1000).
- **Capitalization**: Prefer sentence case for titles/CTAs. Keep consistent across UI and running copy.

**Geography & Proper Nouns**
- **Geo-terms**: Use official, widely used local names. Order: **Country > Region > City**.
- **Airport Names**: Do not add the word “Airport” (機場) if it duplicates context (e.g., if the placeholder context already implies the airport). Ensure consistency via official sources.
- **Transliteration**: Use official standards. Do not invent new spellings. Prioritize well-known local names over literal transliterations.

## Translation Guidelines by Content Type

- **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. Use consistent terminology.
- **Marketing**: Natural, fluid, inspirational. Focus on traveller benefits (time, money, discovery). Keep promises realistic.
- **SEO**: Natural language, no keyword stuffing. Clear headings.
- **Google Ads**: Concise, benefit-driven, active voice. No clickbait or unprovable superlatives (e.g., "cheapest ever").
- **App Store**: Highlight benefits (search, compare, track). No guarantees.
- **Editorial/Blog**: Factual, practical, descriptive.
- **Emails/Notifications**: Short, clear, friendly. Clear action path.
- **Legal/Policies**: Precise, neutral, literal. No emotion or idioms.

## Skyscanner-Specific Brand Rules

**Brand Identity**
- **"Skyscanner"**: Do not translate. Do not add articles. Use as-is.

**Restricted Terms**
- **"Best"**: **Do not use** unless accompanied by a disclaimer explaining measurement. Use subjective alternatives (e.g., "amazing deals") when the source implies quality rather than an objective "best".
- **"Discount"**: **Never use**. Skyscanner does not offer discounts; partners do. Use neutral phrasing (e.g., "You are offered a discount"). Do not imply Skyscanner provides the discount, as this is legally incorrect.

**Sustainability Content**
- **Avoid Greenwashing**: Never imply a flight/travel option is "green," "eco," "sustainable," or "planet-friendly."
- **Comparatives**: Use relative statements (e.g., "X is more sustainable than Y").
- **Transparency**: Base claims on measurable, verified data.
- **Imagery**: Avoid nature-themed imagery that implies aviation is harmless.

## Writing & Style Rules

- **Active Voice**: Use everywhere. Avoid passive constructions.
- **Anglicisms**: Avoid unless the English term is widely expected/understood (e.g., established brand names).
- **Abbreviations/Acronyms**: Use only if universally understood. Define on first use if confusing. Do not invent abbreviations.
- **Formality**: Use consistent T/V (politeness) levels. Do not switch forms unless legal text requires it.
- **Emojis**: Use sparingly. Delete if culturally inappropriate or unclear. Never use in legal/serious contexts.
