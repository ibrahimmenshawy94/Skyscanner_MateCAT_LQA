# Italian Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

*   **Tone of Voice**: **"Geeky Travel Pal"** (Playful, Geeky, Helpful). If forced to choose, **prioritize Helpful**.
*   **Formality**: Always use the **informal "tu"** for all direct user communication. Use impersonal constructions only for Legal/Policies.
*   **Inclusivity**: Use gender-neutral, respectful language. Avoid stereotypes, clichés, and assumptions about travel partners or abilities.
*   **Consistency**: Maintain consistent terminology across all product surfaces.
*   **Legal/Claims**: **Never use unsubstantiated superlatives** (e.g., "best", "cheapest"). **Never claim Skyscanner offers a "discount"** (discounts come from partners). Avoid "greenwashing" in sustainability content.

---

## 1. Tone and Voice

### 1.1 Playful
*   Conversational, lively rhythm; write as if talking to a friend.
*   Focus on simple tips and "travel hacks".
*   **Avoid**: Corporate/management speak, overly stiff phrases, "trying too hard" to be funny.

### 1.2 Geeky
*   Enthusiastic but controlled.
*   Explain data/details in a human way.
*   Break down complex info into simple steps.
*   **Sound like**: A knowledgeable friend, not a lecturer.

### 1.3 Helpful
*   **User-first**: Focus on user needs/benefits, not the company.
*   **Honesty**: Apologize sincerely if at fault. If not at fault, show empathy. Focus on solutions, not feelings.
*   **Simplicity**: Short sentences, simple structure. Use "question then solution" format.

---

## 2. Technical Specifications

### 2.1 URL Requirements
*   **No spaces**: Use hyphens (`-`).
*   **Lowercase**: All letters must be lowercase.
*   **Characters**: No special characters. Use basic Latin letters.

### 2.2 Placeholders and Variables
*   **Never translate, delete, or change** placeholders (e.g., `<location>`).
*   Keep exact syntax and order. Insert spaces if needed.
*   **Grammar workaround**: If a placeholder causes syntax issues (e.g., city/country variability), rephrase the sentence:
    *   *Example*: Instead of "Goditi il weekend a <location>", use `<location>: goditi questo weekend`.

### 2.3 Dates and Formatting
*   **Date Format**: `dd/mm/yyyy`.
*   **Time**: 24-hour format (e.g., `14:30`).

### 2.4 Abbreviations
*   Spell out fully the first time (e.g., "Airport Code (AC)").
*   **Italian specific**: Use periods for abbreviations (ecc., pag., cap.). If a sentence ends with an abbreviation, do not add a second period.

---

## 3. Linguistic Guidelines

### 3.1 Punctuation
*   **Semicolons**: Only for complex, long sentences. Avoid if possible.
*   **Exclamation/Question marks**: Never use more than one.
*   **Hyphens**: Replace with commas or parentheses. Avoid parentheses in email subject lines.
*   **Quotes**: Use double straight quotes (`" "`). Punctuation goes inside quotes; period goes outside.
*   **Apostrophes**: Use straight apostrophe (`'`), not curly.
*   **Ellipsis**: Do not overuse. Capitalize after ellipsis only if it replaces the end of a sentence.
*   **Ampersands**: Do not use (`&`) unless part of a brand name.
*   **Euphonic "d"**: Use only between identical vowels (e.g., *ad Atene*, *ed ecco*). Keep established exceptions (e.g., *ad esempio*). Do not use "od".

### 4.2 Capitalization
*   **Titles/Headers**: Use sentence case (capitalize only the first word and proper nouns).
*   **Dates**: Months and days are lowercase (except for specific events, e.g., *Venerdì santo*).

### 4.3 Call to Action (CTA)
*   Use **imperative mood**.
*   Use **active voice** (clear, direct, natural).

### 4.4 Anglicisms
*   Avoid English terms if a widely accepted, natural Italian equivalent exists.

---

## 5. Content-Specific Guidelines

### 5.1 User Interface (UI)
*   Prioritize clarity over playfulness.
*   One idea per string.
*   **Consistency**: Use standard terms across the entire app (e.g., "Avvisi di prezzo" consistently).

### 5.2 Marketing
*   Natural, fluid, and inspirational.
*   Focus on benefits: time, money, discovery.

### 5.3 SEO
*   Prioritize natural language. Avoid keyword stuffing.
*   Use clear, descriptive headings.

### 5.4 Google Ads / App Store
*   **Strictly concise**. Focus on direct benefits.
*   Avoid: Clickbait, "best" claims, superlatives, guarantees, unnecessary punctuation.

### 5.5 Legal / Policies
*   **Tone**: Precise, neutral, emotionless.
*   No jokes or idioms.
*   No added promises/interpretations.

---

## 6. Brand and Feature Rules

### 6.1 Skyscanner Brand
*   Name must always be "Skyscanner".
*   Do not translate or add articles unless grammatically required.

### 6.2 Feature Naming
*   Focus on meaning and user clarity, not literal translation.
*   Check local market conventions before finalizing.

### 6.3 Best and Superlatives
*   **Avoid** "best" unless accompanied by a disclaimer explaining measurement.
*   Use subjective alternatives like "epic prices" or "amazing deals".

### 6.4 Discounts
*   **Never** say "Skyscanner offers a discount".
*   If a partner offers a discount, use neutral phrasing: "You are offered a discount".
*   Do not use the word "discount" for price drops or deals; these are market fluctuations.

### 6.5 Sustainability
*   **Avoid greenwashing**.
*   **Comparatives only**: Use "X is more sustainable than Y", not absolute claims like "green" or "ecological".
*   Avoid vague terms: "naturale", "non tossico", "rispettoso dell'ambiente".
*   Never imply aviation is environmentally harmless.

---

## 7. Cultural & Accessibility

### 7.1 Geo-terms & Airports
*   Use Google Maps spelling.
*   **Do not translate** airport names (e.g., don't add "Aeroporto" if not in the source).

### 7.2 Emojis & Imagery
*   Only use emojis if present in source.
*   Never use emojis in legal/policy or serious incident messaging.
*   Flag imagery (gestures, food, clothing, landmarks) that may be culturally inappropriate.

### 7.3 Accessibility
*   Write clear, plain, natural Italian.
*   Avoid unnecessary jargon or complex structures.
*   Ensure link text and CTAs are descriptive when read out of context.

## 8. Quick Reference: Do/Don't Examples

| Do | Don't |
| :--- | :--- |
| Con Skyscanner puoi confrontare... | Non serve essere esperti... (Too wordy) |
| Gli utenti possono ora consultare... | Finalmente puoi confrontare i voli... (Too informal for this context) |
| Conosciamo i limiti di peso... | Stiamo attenti a OGNI SINGOLO GRAMMO! (Too dramatic) |
| Vuoi scoprire il giorno più economico? | Se stai cercando di capire... (Too long/confusing) |
| Ti proponiamo i migliori prezzi... | Cerchiamo migliaia di opzioni... (Passive/Weak) |
| Hai bisogno di aiuto? Contatta... | Mettiti in contatto con il team... (Too conversational) |
| voli economici | Voli-economici (Incorrect casing) |
| Perfetto per gruppi, coppie o famiglie. | Perfetto per mamme, papà e bambini. (Stereotyping) |
| Strade ripide, difficile accesso... | Il quartiere non è adatto ai disabili. (Insensitive) |
| Prenota ora (Sentence case/Imperative) | Prenota Ora (Title case) |
| Mostra i voli | Diamo un'occhiata ad alcuni fantastici voli... (Too much filler) |
