# DE Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

*   **Tone Priority**: 1. Helpful, 2. Geeky, 3. Playful.
*   **Formality**: Use informal singular **"du"** (lowercase: *du, dir, deine*).
*   **Accuracy & Compliance**: Adhere to **Duden** for grammar and spelling.
*   **Superlatives**: Do not claim "best prices" or "cheapest" without clear, measurable disclaimers. Avoid clickbait.
*   **Sustainability**: No greenwashing. Use comparatives (e.g., "lower carbon emissions") instead of absolute labels (e.g., "green," "eco-friendly," "sustainable").
*   **Voice**: Use **active voice**. Avoid noun-heavy structures (*Nominalstil*).
*   **Brand**: Never translate "Skyscanner".

## Tone and Voice

### The "Geeky Travel Pal" Persona
*   **Playful**: Conversational, light, natural rhythm. Use rhyme, alliteration, and metaphors only if they localize naturally; do not force. Avoid "fake-smart" corporate speak or "LOL-funny" attempts. **Clarity > Wordplay**.
*   **Geeky**: Passionate about data and patterns, explained humanly. Enthusiastic but controlled. Break complex info into simple steps.
*   **Helpful**: First priority. If a choice conflicts, choose **Helpful**. Be honest, upfront, and transparent.

## General Writing Guidelines

### Language & Grammar
*   **Inclusive Language**: Avoid stereotypes (gender, age, family, background). Prefer neutral terms: *Alle Reisenden* (not *Jeder Nutzer*); *2 Gäste/Übernachtungsgäste* (not *2 Besucher*). Use generic masculine only if no neutral term exists and space is constrained.
*   **Anglicisms**: Use only if widely understood (e.g., *Deal*). Avoid unnecessary ones.
*   **Abbreviations**: Use only if widely known (airport codes). Write out full term on first mention if ambiguous.
*   **Active Voice**: Always prioritize active, direct sentences.
*   **Capitalization**: Follow standard German rules (nouns, first word of sentence). No Title Case.

### Punctuation & Formatting
*   **Commas**: No Oxford comma.
*   **Ellipsis**: Exactly three dots (`...`), preceded by a non-breaking space.
*   **Quotes**: Use `„xyz“` (99-66 rule). Single quotes for nested: `„xyz ‚xyz‘ xyz.“`
*   **Hyphens**: Use for compound nouns/numbers (e.g., *80-prozentig*). Do not use if the combination is a standard German noun (e.g., *Feedback*).
*   **Dashes**: Use for thought strings or time spans (no spaces for time spans).
*   **Ampersands**: Use only for brand names or UI space constraints (buttons). Use a non-breaking space before `&`.
*   **Apostrophes**: Must look like a 9. Only use when grammatically required.
*   **Percentage/Units**: Use a non-breaking space between number and symbol (`20 %`, `1.500 km`).

### Numbers, Dates, and Time
*   **Writing Numbers**: Write out numbers up to 12. Use digits for 13+.
*   **Formatting**: Use decimal commas (`,`) and thousand-separator dots (`.`). 
*   **DIN 5008**: Follow guidelines for date/time formatting.

### URL Rules
*   **Format**: Lowercase, no spaces, no special characters/accents.
*   **Separators**: Use hyphens (`-`) to separate words.

### Placeholders & Tags
*   **Rule**: Never translate, delete, or change placeholders (e.g., `@@city@@`). 
*   **Context**: Ensure grammatical compatibility in German (word order, prepositions) when inserting tags. Use spaces before/after tags to prevent concatenation.

## Content-Type Specific Guidelines

*   **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. Consistent terminology (e.g., always "Price Alerts").
*   **Marketing**: Natural, fluid, inspirational. Prioritize traveller benefits.
*   **SEO**: Natural language, no keyword stuffing. Clear, descriptive headings.
*   **Google Ads**: Concise. Benefit-driven. Strict character limits. No unprovable claims.
*   **App Store**: Highlight features (search, compare, track). No superlatives.
*   **Editorial/Blog**: Warm, descriptive, factual. Balance inspiration with practical tips.
*   **Legal/Policies**: Precise, neutral, consistent. No emotional language or jokes.

## Specific Terminology & Usage

*   **Skyscanner Brand**: Never translate. No articles unless required by grammar.
*   **Discounts**: Skyscanner does not offer discounts (partners do). Never say "Skyscanner offers a discount". Use: "You are offered a discount."
*   **Airport Names**: Don't add "Airport" if already present in source. Use official sources for naming.
*   **Feature Names**: Focus on clarity over literal translation. Must be short, scannable, and natural.
*   **Sustainability**: 
    *   Prefer: "X has 20% lower carbon emissions than a typical flight."
    *   Avoid: "This is a sustainable flight" or "greenest option."
    *   Use metrics/data-backed claims only.

## Do's and Don'ts Summary

*   **Tone**: 
    *   *Do*: "Möchtest du wissen, an welchem Tag du am günstigsten fliegen kannst? Wir helfen dir gerne weiter."
    *   *Don't*: Overly casual/forced humor ("Schnall dich an und schnapp dir die Ersparnisse!").
*   **Clarity**:
    *   *Do*: "Du findest die besten Flugpreise aus Tausenden Optionen."
    *   *Don't*: "Wir durchsuchen Tausende von Flugoptionen, damit du die besten Preise kriegst."
*   **Conciseness**:
    *   *Do*: "Jetzt buchen."
    *   *Don't*: "Leg jetzt los und buche."
*   **Terminology**:
    *   *Do*: "Günstige Deals."
    *   *Don't*: "Günstige Offers" (mixed languages).
*   **Legal/Policy**:
    *   *Do*: "Dies ist der beste Preis, den wir auf unserer Plattform bei unserer weitreichenden Suche gefunden haben."
    *   *Don't*: "Dies ist der beste Preis für deinen Flug" (implies a universal superlative that cannot be substantiated).
*   **Sensitivity**:
    *   *Do*: "Es gibt einige steile Straßen, die für Reisende mit eingeschränkter Mobilität eine Herausforderung darstellen können."
    *   *Don't*: "Diese Gegend ist nicht für Behinderte geeignet."
*   **Formatting**:
    *   *Do*: "Paris Charles de Gaulle"
    *   *Don't*: "Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport" (redundant).
    *   *Do*: Use ellipsis `…` (Alt+0133). 
    *   *Don't*: `...` (3 individual dots).
