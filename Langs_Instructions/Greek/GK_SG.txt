# EL Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

**Core Identity**: Skyscanner is a "Geeky Travel Pal". Tone must be **playful, geeky, and helpful**.
**Priority Hierarchy**: Always be **helpful** first. If a rule clashes, helpfulness takes precedence.
**Formality**: Use the formal "εσείς" and "σας" consistently across all content (UI, marketing, emails). Avoid switching to informal.
**Politeness**: Do not use "Παρακαλώ" ("Please"). The formal address is sufficiently polite; extra politeness sounds apologetic or stiff.
**Brand Identity**: Refer to Skyscanner as **"η Skyscanner"** (feminine article). Never use "το Skyscanner".

---

## Language-Specific Grammar & Punctuation

### Punctuation
- **Commas**: Use after salutations, to differentiate sentences, for decimals (e.g., 1,50 €), and explanatory juxtapositions. Avoid middle-of-sentence commas if possible for readability.
- **Ellipsis**: Use “…” (Alt+0133), never three separate dots.
- **Exclamation Marks**: Limit to one. Avoid excessive usage; never use in legal or serious/policy contexts.
- **Quotation Marks**: Use standard straight quotes ("..."). Use for UI references.
- **Hyphens**: No spaces (e.g., “σιγά-σιγά”, “45-65 ετών”).
- **En Dash**: Use as minus sign with spaces (e.g., “15 – 6 = 9”). Alt+0150.
- **Em Dash**: Use sparingly (prefer period/comma/parentheses). If used, add space before first dash and after second; no spaces between dashes and the clause. Alt+0151.
- **Full Stops**: Break long sentences to improve readability.

### Grammar Rules
- **Final -ν**: 
    - Drop for feminine "την", "μην", "αυτήν" before β, γ, δ, ζ, θ, λ, μ, ν, ρ, σ, φ, χ. 
    - Keep for masculine "τον", "αυτόν", "έναν" regardless of the next letter.
- **Interrogatives**: Use accents: “πού” (where), “πώς” (how).
- **Comparison**: “Σαν” (parallel), “Ως” (attribute). Use appropriately.
- **Numbers**: “Μία” (numerical adjective) vs “μια” (indefinite article).

## Technical & Formatting Guidelines

### URLs
- **Format**: All lowercase.
- **Characters**: Latin only, no special/extended characters.
- **Separators**: Use hyphens (-) only. No spaces.
- **Greek Exception**: Transliterate Greek words into Latin characters.

### Tags, Placeholders, & Variables
- **Integrity**: Never translate, delete, or change placeholders/tags. Maintain exact order.
- **Grammar Safety**: Placeholders cannot be conjugated (they remain Nominative). Adjust surrounding sentence structure (rephrase) so the sentence remains grammatically correct regardless of the substituted value (e.g., avoid direct declension dependence).

### Capitalization
- **Style**: Use sentence case for almost everything. Avoid Title Case unless specified for marketing.
- **Months/Days**: Lowercase.
- **UI Elements/Glossary**: Follow consistent pattern. If it’s a branded feature name, use the established capitalisation.

## Translation & Tone Guidelines

### Tone Implementation
- **Goal**: Sound like a knowledgeable friend, not a lecture.
- **Playfulness**: Use rhyme, alliteration, metaphors, and vivid adjectives *only if they occur naturally in Greek*. Do not force wordplay.
- **Geekiness**: Break down complex data into simple, human-friendly steps.
- **Avoid**: "Fake-smart" corporate speak, overly stiff phrases, "LOL-funny" attempts, and passive voice.

### Inclusive Language
- **Strategy**: Avoid stereotypes (gender, age, background, ability). 
- **Gender**: Use gender-neutral where possible. When gendered endings are required, use “αυτός/ή” or “-ος/-η”. Minimize overuse of “τον/την” and “ο/η”.
- **Disability**: Use terms like "ΑμεΑ" or "άτομο με αναπηρία". Avoid "άτομο με ειδικές ανάγκες". 

### CTAs & Voice
- **Active Voice**: Always prefer active voice for headlines and CTAs.
- **Imperatives**: Use for CTAs to encourage action (e.g., "Κάντε κράτηση", not "Κράτηση"). 
- **Exceptions**: Use noun phrases for non-active buttons (e.g., "Ακύρωση").

### Terminology & Policy
- **"Best"**: Never claim "Best prices/deals" without a visible disclaimer explaining the methodology. If no disclaimer, use subjective alternatives like "epic prices" or "amazing deals".
- **"Discount"**: Never use for Skyscanner offers. Skyscanner does not offer discounts. Refer to "deals", "price drops", or "offers". If a partner offers a discount, phrase as "You are offered a discount" (neutral).
- **Sustainability**: Avoid greenwashing. 
    - **No absolutes**: Do not use "green", "eco", "sustainable", "planet-friendly".
    - **Comparatives only**: Use "X emits 20% less carbon than Y".
    - **Factual**: Base claims on data only.

### Geographic Names
- **Transliteration**: Use official/widely used local names.
- **Consistency**: Do not mix systems. 
- **Airports**: Do not add "Airport" if the name includes the place name (avoid duplication). Only localise if a standard Greek version exists (e.g., Paris Charles de Gaulle).

## Content Type Specifications

- **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string.
- **Marketing**: Natural, fluid, inspirational. Focus on benefits (time/money saving).
- **SEO**: Use natural keywords. Avoid keyword stuffing.
- **Google Ads**: Very concise, no clickbait. No superlatives or unprovable claims.
- **Editorial/Blog**: Warmer, factual, practical. Balance inspiration with tips.
- **Legal/Terms**: Precision, neutrality, consistency. No emotional language.
