# AR Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
- **Tone Hierarchy**: Prioritize **Helpful** first, followed by **Geeky** (data-driven, expert) and **Playful** (human, conversational). If a conflict arises, prioritize **Helpful**.
- **Placeholders/Variables**: **MUST NOT be translated, deleted, or changed**. Keep exactly as in source (including spacing before/after tags).
- **URLs**: **Must be lowercase, contain no spaces (use hyphens), and use only Latin characters**.
- **Accuracy**: Never use unsubstantiated superlatives (e.g., "best", "cheapest ever"). Use "best" only with disclaimers or subjective terms like "epic prices".
- **Sustainability**: Never use greenwashing terms (e.g., "eco", "green", "guilt-free"). Use comparatives ("X reduces emissions by Y% compared to Z") rather than absolutes.
- **Brand**: The name **Skyscanner** must **never** be translated or adapted. Do not add articles unless required by local grammar.

## Tone and Voice
- **Helpful**: Focus on traveller needs. Be honest, upfront, and apologize sincerely (no "we apologize for the inconvenience"). Use "pose question → offer solution" format.
- **Geeky**: Enthusiastic but controlled. Love data/details but explain in human terms. Break complex info into simple steps.
- **Playful**: Human, conversational, natural. 
    - *Tools*: Use rhyme, alliteration, metaphors if they occur naturally.
    - *Restrictions*: Avoid puns, corporate speak, stiff phrases, and "LOL-funny" attempts.
    - *Clarity*: If wordplay compromises meaning or sounds forced in Arabic, **discard it**.

## Localization Strategy
- **Meaning over literalism**: Prioritize natural phrasing and local culture over matching English syntax.
- **Inclusive Language**: Avoid stereotypes (gender, age, background). Use neutral, precise terms. Do not assume user demographics.
- **Anglicisms**: Avoid unless widely accepted (e.g., specific brand names).
- **Abbreviations**: Use only if widely understood. Write full term + (abbr) on first mention.

## Technical Guidelines

### URLs
- **Format**: `lowercase`, `hyphen` separator, no special chars.
- **Encoding**: Keep entirely in Latin characters (do not transliterate/translate URL content).

### Placeholders & Tags
- **Handling**: Keep in exact source order. Ensure natural grammar around the tag. Add spaces before/after tags if needed to prevent text concatenation.
- **Validation**: If the tool renders placeholders as text, do not translate the content inside them.

## Arabic Language Guidelines

### Punctuation and Spacing
- **Comma (،)**: Arabic comma points upward (opposite of English `,`). No space before, one space after. Never start a line with it.
- **Question Mark (؟)**: Must use the Arabic version (؟), not the English (?).
- **Exclamation Marks**: Use sparingly. Only for surprise/amazement, not for "important" or "warning". Prefer periods/colons.
- **Quotation Marks (" ")**: Enclose direct speech, titles, UI texts, and proper nouns (compensates for lack of capitals). No space between quote and text. 
- **Hyphens/Dashes**:
    - **Dialogue**: Use dash (—) at line start for speaker changes.
    - **Lists/Ranges**: Use hyphen/dash for number separation (e.g., 1- الأول or 18-28). Avoid to join words or break words at line ends.
- **Full Stops**: No space before, one space after.

### Formatting & Dates
- **Capitalization**: Avoid Title Case. Use Sentence Case (unless otherwise standard).
- **Dates**: Day/Month/Year (DD/MM/YYYY).
- **Months**: Use Arabized Western names: (يناير، فبراير، مارس، أبريل، مايو، يونيو، يوليو، أغسطس، سبتمبر، أكتوبر، نوفمبر، ديسمبر).
- **Time**: 12-hour clock + period indicators (صباحًا/مساءً).

### Address Policy
- **Formality**: Arabic lacks direct T/V distinction. Convey politeness via pronouns and specific phrases. Maintain a consistent, helpful friend tone.

## Linguistic Guidelines

### Toponyms and Transliteration
- **Popular places**: Translate to the commonly known version (e.g., Eiffel Tower → برج إيفل).
- **Unknown places**: Keep untranslated or transliterate if standard.
- **City names**: Do not translate unless a standard Arabic version exists. Keep as source if searchable/unknown.
- **Historical Sites**: Use standard Arabic translations (e.g., Great Wall of China → سور الصين العظيم).
- **Airport Names**: 
    - **Structure**: Transliterate proper names (Miami → ميامي), translate descriptive terms (International → دولي).
    - **Avoid Duplication**: Do not add "airport" if already in the name; translate as "مطار [Name]".
    - **Standard**: Follow IATA/ICAO codes.

### Content Type Rules
- **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string.
- **Marketing**: Natural, fluid, inspirational. Focus on traveller benefits (saving time/money).
- **SEO**: Natural language, no keyword stuffing. Descriptive titles.
- **Google Ads**: Concise, benefit-driven, direct CTA. Avoid clickbait.
- **App Store**: Highlight features (search, compare). No superlatives.
- **Editorial/Blog**: Factual but warm. Balanced advice.
- **Legal/Policies**: Precise, neutral. No idioms/jokes.
- **Emails/Push**: Short subject lines, clear next steps.

### Sustainability
- **Principles**: Use comparatives (not absolutes). Use measurable data. 
- **Avoid**: "Green", "eco", "natural", "non-toxic", "clean", "planet-friendly", "guilt-free".
- **Imagery**: Do not reinforce nature-themed metaphors that imply aviation is harmless.

## Brand & Feature Naming
- **Skyscanner**: Never translate the brand name.
- **Feature Names**: Focus on clarity. If a literal translation is confusing (e.g., "Packages"), use the local equivalent (e.g., "Package Holidays"). 
- **Checklist for names**:
    1. Scanability/Length.
    2. Clarity of function.
    3. Local familiarity (check competitors).

## Glossary/Usage Reminders
- **Discount**: **Never** use "discount" (خصم) unless specifically referring to a partner offer. Use "deal", "price drops", or "offers" for fluctuations.
- **Best**: Avoid superlatives. If English says "best", verify if it is an objective claim. If subjective, use terms like "epic prices" or "amazing deals".
- **Active Voice**: Use for all CTAs and headlines. Use imperative forms (e.g., سجِّل الدخول, احجز الآن).

## Quality Control (Don'ts)
- Do not mix English/Local words in a phrase (e.g., "flight deals super baratos").
- Do not use clickbait (e.g., "You won't believe this offer!!!").
- Do not use emojis to replace keywords or in serious/legal content.
- Do not make assumptions about traveler groupings or labels.
- Do not use internal acronyms (e.g., PNR) without explanation.
- Do not translate meta-labels if they are system variables.
