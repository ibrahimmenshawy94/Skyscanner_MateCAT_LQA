# Catalan Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
- **Tone**: Maintain the "Geeky Travel Pal" persona: Playful, Geeky, Helpful. **If in conflict, prioritize Helpful.**
- **Clarity**: Always prefer clarity over wordplay.
- **Sustainability**: Avoid greenwashing. Never use terms like ‘eco’, ‘sustainable’, ‘green’, or ‘carbon-neutral’ unless backed by measurable data. Use comparatives (e.g., "emits 20% less carbon") rather than absolutes.
- **Addressing Users**: Use "tu" for general content; use "vós" for legal/policy content only.
- **Variables/Placeholders**: **Never translate or change tags.** Maintain exact syntax/spacing. If Catalan grammar (apostrophes) conflicts with a variable, rephrase the sentence or use brackets/colons before the tag.
- **No Discounts**: Skyscanner does not offer discounts. Never imply "we offer" a discount. Use neutral language like "You are offered a discount" or describe price drops/deals.

---

## Tone of Voice: Geeky Travel Pal
- **Playful**: Conversational tone (talking to a friend), lively rhythm. Use "travel hacks" and simple tips. 
    - *Tools*: Rhyme, alliteration, metaphors (use sparingly).
    - *Avoid*: Corny puns, "fake-smart" corporate speak, "LOL-funny" attempts, overly formal/stiff phrases.
- **Geeky**: Enthusiastic, data-driven, precise. Explain complex details simply. Tone should be a knowledgeable friend, not a lecturer.
- **Helpful**: Put the traveler first. Start with benefits/instructions. 
    - *Apologies*: Drop playfulness, show empathy, be transparent, focus on solutions. Avoid "sorry/unfortunately" where possible.

---

## General Writing & Translation Guidelines
- **Inclusive Language**: Avoid stereotypes (gender, age, ability, family status). Use neutral, precise terms. No slash/asterisk forms (e.g., no *viatgers/es). 
- **Active Voice**: Mandatory in headlines/CTAs.
- **CTAs**: Short, direct, imperative. **Avoid "ara"**. Focus on user action.
- **Anglicisms**: Use only if widely accepted. Avoid if a clear Catalan equivalent exists.
- **Abbreviations**: Use only if widely understood (e.g., PNR). Define acronyms: "Full term (ACRONYM)".
- **Best/Superlatives**: Do not claim "best prices/deals". Use subjective claims ("preus èpics", "ofertes increïbles") or explain how "best" is measured.

---

## Technical & Formatting Rules

### URLs
- **Format**: All lowercase, no special/extended characters (replace with basic Latin).
- **Separator**: Use hyphens (`-`) instead of spaces.

### Punctuation & Spacing
- **Exclamation Marks**: Use sparingly. Avoid multiple marks (!!!).
- **Question Marks**: Use in titles containing questions.
- **Quotation Marks**: Use angular (« ») first, then straight (" "), then single (' '). Do not use for UI terms.
- **Dashes**: Avoid unless necessary.
- **Headers**: No final period.
- **Bullet Points**: Period only if full sentences. No ending comma.
- **Alt Text/Links**: Alt text ends with a period; links do not.
- **Emphasis**: Use bold/italics. Avoid caps or extra punctuation for emphasis.
- **Colons**: Do not capitalize the word following a colon unless it starts a new line.

### Capitalization
- **Titles/CTAs**: Sentence case (Capitalize first word + proper nouns).
- **Product Names**: Consistent across UI/copy. Use sentence case unless branded.

---

## Catalan-Specific Linguistics
- **Place Names/Transliteration**: Use official/widely used local names. Check Nomenclàtor/ÉsAdir first. Do not add "airport" unless it's the official name or needed for context.
- **Apostrophes (Placeholders)**: If a tag causes an apostrophe issue, rephrase or use brackets/colons.
- **Example**: `@@city@@` -> `Vola des de @@city@@` (Ensure article/preposition compatibility).

---

## Content-Type Guidelines

### User Interface (UI)
- Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string.
- Consistent terminology (e.g., always "Price Alerts").

### Marketing
- Creative, fluid, inspirational.
- Focus on traveller benefits (saving time, money, effort).

### SEO
- Natural language only (no keyword stuffing).
- Descriptive headings/titles.

### Google Ads
- Extremely concise.
- Direct benefits and CTA.
- No clickbait/unprovable claims (e.g., "the best").

### App Store
- Highlight main benefits (search, compare, track).
- Simple phrases. Avoid superlatives/guarantees.

### Editorial & Blog
- Warm, descriptive, factual, practical.
- Adapt content to local relevance.

### Emails/Push Notifications
- Short subject lines.
- Clear next-step actions.

### Legal/Policies
- Accurate, precise, neutral.
- **Treatment**: Use "vós".
- No emotional language, jokes, or interpretations.

---

## Branding & Terminology
- **Skyscanner Brand**: Do not translate or add articles. Always use "Skyscanner".
- **Feature Names**: Focus on meaning/clarity. Use local travel provider equivalents if "literal" is misleading (e.g., "Packages" -> "package holidays" if needed).
- **Sustainability (Do's/Don'ts)**:
    - *Do*: Use comparatives (e.g., "X is more sustainable than Y"). Use data (e.g., "20% less carbon").
    - *Don't*: Use labels like ‘natural’, ‘eco’, ‘green’, ‘net’, ‘respectuós amb el planeta’, or ‘sense culpes’. Avoid metaphors implying aviation is environmentally harmless.
