# Thai Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

- **Tone**: Maintain a "Geeky Travel Pal" persona: **Playful, Geeky, Helpful**.
- **Helpful Priority**: Always prioritize "Helpful" over Playful/Geeky.
- **Skyscanner Brand**: Always use the English spelling "**Skyscanner**". Never translate or adapt the name.
- **Greenwashing**: **Never** imply environmental superiority (e.g., avoid "green," "eco," "sustainable," "planet-friendly"). Use factual, comparative data only.
- **Discounts**: **Never** use the word "discount." Skyscannner does not offer them. Use "deal" or "price drop" instead, and attribute to partners if necessary.
- **"Best" Claims**: Use only with a disclaimer explaining the measurement. Otherwise, avoid superlatives.
- **Consistency**: Use standardized terminology across all platforms (UI, Marketing, Help).

## Tone of Voice

Our goal: Readers should feel they are talking to a smart, friendly, helpful travel companion.

- **Playful**: Use light, natural energy. Use conversational Thai that feels like talking to a friend. Avoid robotic or stiff phrasing.
- **Geeky**: Show expertise/data, but keep it accessible. Enthusiasm must be controlled; avoid lecturing.
- **Helpful**: Foundation of our communication. Prioritize traveller needs over brand.
    - **Apologies**: If our fault, apologize sincerely once. Focus on the solution. Avoid "We apologize for the inconvenience."
    - **Simplicity**: Keep sentences short, simple, and structured (Pose question → Offer solution).

## Linguistic & Formatting Standards

### Capitalization and Emphasis
- **No Caps in Thai**: Thai does not have uppercase/lowercase.
- **Visual Anchors**: To distinguish feature names/tools, enclose them in "quotation marks" or use **bold**.
- **Sentence Case**: Use sentence case for Latin-script text (unless marketing/ads). Do not use title case.
- **Emphasis**: Prefer **bold** or white space over ALL CAPS or exclamation marks.

### Punctuation & Spacing
- **Full Stops**: **Do not use** at the end of complete sentences, paragraphs, titles, headings, or bullet points. Use only as a decimal point.
- **Exclamation Marks**: Reserve for urgent CTAs or critical alerts (flash sales). Use sparingly to maintain impact.
- **Commas**: Use only to segment lists or complex series.
- **Ellipsis (...)**: Use for omitted text in lists or ongoing processes (loading).
- **Hyphens**: Use the shortest dash for connecting words.
- **Dashes (En Dash)**: Use for ranges (e.g., dates/prices). **Do not** use to emphasize or provide extra info (rephrase instead).

### Addressing Users
- **Tone**: Friendly, respectful "travel pal."
- **Pronouns**: Omit "you" in UI/CTAs for conciseness. Use "you" only in help content or personalized emails.
- **Brand**: Use "we" to refer to Skyscanner to imply partnership.

### Transliteration & Names
- **Standard**: Follow the **Royal Society of Thailand (RST)** standards.
- **Consistency**: Do not mix transliteration systems. Use established exonyms.
- **Airport Names**: Use official **IATA** sources. **Never** add the word "airport" in the translation (it is added via placeholders).

## Technical Specifications

### URLs
- **No spaces**: Use hyphens (-) if separators are needed.
- **Format**: All lowercase.
- **Characters**: Latin characters only. No special/extended characters.

### Placeholders & Variables
- **Do not translate, delete, or change** placeholders/tags (e.g., `@@city@@`).
- **Syntax**: Placeholders act as modifiers. In Thai, modifiers often follow the noun. Move placeholders to the correct grammatical position after the noun.
- **Grammar Check**: Ensure sentence remains grammatically correct when variables are populated. Insert necessary Thai particles or clarifying words (e.g., "price of @@price@@") before placeholders if required for natural flow.

## Content Type Guidelines

### User Interface (UI)
- **Priority**: Clarity over playfulness.
- **Format**: Short, action-focused. One idea per string. Use consistent terminology.

### Marketing
- **Goal**: Inspirational, natural, fluid.
- **Scope**: Creative freedom allowed, but keep promises realistic. Focus on traveller benefits (saving time/money).

### SEO
- **Style**: Natural language. Avoid keyword stuffing.
- **Structure**: Clear, descriptive headings. Concise meta titles.

### Google Ads
- **Constraint**: Strict character limits.
- **Rule**: Direct benefits and CTAs only. No clickbait (no "#1", "best"). No extra punctuation.

### App Store
- **Rule**: Focus on core features (search/compare/track). Avoid superlatives/guarantees. Simple, natural phrasing for screenshots.

### Legal & Policies
- **Tone**: Precise, neutral.
- **Rule**: No jokes, idioms, or emotional language. No interpretations outside the source text.

## Restricted Terminology

| Term | Usage Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **"Discount"** | **Forbidden**. Use "deal," "price drop," or local equivalent. |
| **"Best"** | Use **only** if accompanied by a disclaimer explaining the measurement. |
| **"Green/Eco"** | **Forbidden** in sustainability claims. Use objective, comparative, data-backed statements only. |
| **English** | Use only for brand names, technical terms, or industry-standard abbreviations. Avoid "trendy" Anglicisms. |

## Cultural Sensitivity
- **Imagery**: Flag clothing, gestures, food, or landmarks that may be culturally inappropriate or offensive in Thailand.
- **Respect**: Adhere to Thai standards of "politeness, respect, and deference."
- **Emojis**: Use sparingly. Only if they support clarity or brand voice (e.g., ✈️, 💡). Do not replace key words with emojis. Avoid in legal/serious content.
