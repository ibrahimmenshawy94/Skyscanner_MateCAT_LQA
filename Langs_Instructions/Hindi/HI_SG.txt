# Hindi Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

*   **Brand Identity**: Keep `Skyscanner` in English. Never translate or adapt; do not add articles.
*   **Helpful First**: If you must choose between playful, geeky, or helpful, **always prioritize helpfulness**.
*   **Discount Policy**: Skyscanner never offers discounts. Never translate "we offer" or "Skyscanner offers" a discount. Use neutral language like "You are offered a discount."
*   **Sustainability**: **No greenwashing**. Use comparatives (e.g., "X emits less carbon than Y"), never absolutes (e.g., "the most sustainable"). Avoid terms like "eco," "green," "planet-friendly."
*   **Superlatives**: Avoid "best," "cheapest ever," or "most." Use only if a disclaimer exists (e.g., "Best results" with context). Prefer subjective terms like "epic prices."
*   **Placeholders**: Do **not** translate, delete, or change placeholders/variables/tags (e.g., `@@city@@`). Keep exactly as source, with correct spacing.

## Tone of Voice: "Geeky Travel Pal"

*   **Playful**: Use a conversational, rhythmic tone. Avoid corporate/stiff language. Use rhymes/alliteration only if natural. **Clarity always overrides wordplay.**
*   **Geeky**: Share data/details enthusiastically but simply. Sound like a knowledgeable friend, not a lecturer.
*   **Helpful**: Make complex info simple.
    *   **Put travellers first**: Start with benefits/instructions.
    *   **Be honest**: If apologizing, drop the playfulness. State what happened, next steps, and solution. No cold "inconvenience" phrasing.
    *   **Keep it short**: Use simple, active sentence structures.

## Technical & URL Guidelines

*   **URLs**: For Hindi, keep URLs in English. Use standard hyphen `-` for separators. All lowercase.
*   **Placeholders**: Ensure grammatical correctness in Hindi, which may require workarounds.
    *   *Example (EN)*: "Search flights to `@@city@@`"
    *   *Example (HI)*: "`@@city@@` " (Ensure spacing to avoid merging words).

## General Writing Guidelines

### Inclusive Language
*   Avoid stereotypes, unnecessary gender/family/age/background references.
*   Prefer neutral, precise terms.
*   **Gender of Loan Words**: Assign gender as follows (not exhaustive):
    *   **Masculine**: Folder, Audio, Browser, Brand, Button, Calendar, Call, Payment, Click, Connection, Credit, Data, Email, Label, Link, Mobile, Video, Network, Media, Password, App, Computer, Transaction, Access, Template, Process, Device.
    *   **Feminine**: Photo, File, Billing, Report, Post, Rank, Screen, Team, Library, Profile, Site, Cookies, Feed, Insight.

### Formatting & Punctuation
*   **Punctuation**: Stick to English source punctuation for UI.
*   **Emphasis**: Use **bold** or *italics*. Avoid excessive exclamation marks.
*   **Anglicisms**: Avoid unless widely used/expected. Ensure consistency.
*   **Abbreviations/Acronyms**: Only if universally understood (e.g., airport codes). Write full term first, then (abbreviation).
*   **CTAs/Active Voice**: Use active voice. Use imperative verbs for CTAs. Focus on user action.
*   **Numbers**: 
    *   Up to 3 digits: No separator (e.g., 100).
    *   4+ digits: Comma separator (e.g., 1,000; 1,00,000).
*   **Dates/Time**: Use 24-hour format for UI. Leave AM/PM in English for constant-variable situations.
*   **Currency**: Symbol to the left, no space (e.g., ₹30).

### Transliteration & Names
*   **Transliteration**: Use standard official standards. Be consistent. Do not invent spellings. Use established local exonyms.
*   **Airport Names**: Generally transliterate. **Never** add "airport" if it's already in the name; it will be added dynamically by the system.
*   **Geo-terms**: Use the most commonly used, understandable local names.

### Cultural Sensitivity
*   **Emojis/Symbols**: Use sparingly for clarity/tone. Never use in legal/serious/policy content. Replace if culturally inappropriate.
*   **Imagery**: Flag any culturally inappropriate imagery (clothing, gestures, food, etc.).

## Content-Type Specifics

*   **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. Use verbs for buttons/commands; nouns for menus/lists.
*   **Marketing**: Fluid, natural, inspirational. Focus on benefits (time, money, effort).
*   **SEO**: Natural language. Avoid keyword stuffing. Concise headings.
*   **Google Ads**: Very concise, benefit-driven, direct CTA. No "clickbait" or unprovable claims.
*   **App Store**: Highlight benefits. Simple, natural phrases. No superlatives.
*   **Editorial/Blog**: Warmer, descriptive, factual, practical.
*   **Emails/Push**: Short subject lines, helpful body copy. Clear next steps.
*   **Legal**: Precise, neutral. No idioms or emotional language. Do not add interpretations.

## Feature Naming & Terminology

*   **Feature Names**: Prioritize meaning over literal translation. Must be short, clear, and familiar.
    *   *Example*: "Packages" may need to be "Package holidays" to avoid confusion with "parcels."
*   **"Best" Usage**: Use only with a disclaimer explaining measurement. If no disclaimer, use subjective alternatives like "epic" or "amazing."
*   **Sustainability**: Use comparatives ("X emits 20% less carbon than Y"). Avoid "green," "eco," "sustainable," or "clean" unless based on verifiable data. Avoid green symbolism/metaphors.

## Common Do’s and Don’ts

*   **Tone**: 
    *   *Do*: "Need to know the cheapest day to fly? We got you."
    *   *Don't*: Over-complicated explanations or robotic phrasing.
*   **CTA**:
    *   *Do*: "Get in touch with our customer support team if you need help."
    *   *Don't*: "Need help? Get in touch with our customer support team." (Keep CTAs direct).
*   **Formatting**:
    *   *Do*: "city-break"
    *   *Don't*: "citybreak" (Use hyphens for URLs).
*   **Clarity**:
    *   *Do*: "Some streets are steep and may be difficult for travellers with reduced mobility."
    *   *Don't*: "This area isn’t suitable for the disabled." (Avoid labeling).
*   **Consistency**:
    *   *Do*: Use local equivalents for "deal" or "offer."
    *   *Don't*: Mix English and local words in a way that feels trendy but unclear (e.g., "flight deals super baratos").
*   **Legal/Accuracy**:
    *   *Do*: "We do not guarantee the availability or price of any flight until your booking is confirmed."
    *   *Don't*: "We can’t always guarantee availability or price before your booking is confirmed." (Be precise).
