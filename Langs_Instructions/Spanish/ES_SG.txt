# es-ES Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements: Tone & Voice
**Core Persona**: "Geeky Travel Pal."
- **Playful**: Conversational, natural rhythm, light energy. Use puns/metaphors *only* if they feel natural and maintain clarity.
- **Geeky**: Data-driven, expert, inquisitive, human. Simplify complex info.
- **Helpful**: Foundation priority. If rules conflict, **be helpful first**. Make travel information clear, manageable, and simple.

**Formality (T/V Policy)**:
- **General**: Use informal **“tú”**. Refer to Skyscanner as "nosotros/nuestro".
- **Legal**: Use formal **“usted”**.

**Sustainability & Greenwashing**:
- **Avoid**: "Sustainable", "eco", "green", "clean", "planet-friendly", "guilt-free".
- **Requirement**: Use transparent, factual, data-based language. Prefer comparatives ("X emits 20% less carbon than Y") over absolutes ("greenest option").

## Technical & Formatting Standards

### URLs
- **Case**: Always lowercase.
- **Separators**: Use standard hyphen `-`.
- **Characters**: Latin only. No special/extended characters (replace with basic Latin).
- **Format**: `example-url-string`

### Placeholders, Variables & Tags
- **Handling**: **Never translate, delete, or change** tags/variables (e.g., `%{time}`, `@@variable@@`).
- **Syntax**: Keep exact source order. Ensure grammar (e.g., articles, adjectives) in surrounding text agrees with the inserted variable. Use placeholders/workarounds to avoid agreement issues (e.g., "Se ha encontrado 1 @@flight@@" instead of "1 @@flight@@ encontrado").

### Numbers & Dates
- **Format**: DD/MM/YYYY.
- **Time**: 24h format with colon (e.g., `14:00`). Do not use "h" after time; if used, insert a non-breaking space.
- **Decimals**: Use a comma `,` (e.g., `10,5`).
- **Thousands**: Use a non-breaking space (e.g., `10 000`).
- **4-figure numbers**: Do **not** use a separator.
- **Conversational**: Write numbers 0–10 in words (except prices/percentages).

### Punctuation
- **Quotes**: Use angular quotation marks `« »` instead of “ ”.
- **Periods**: Do **not** use full stops in buttons, menu text, or headers.
- **Emphasis**: Use bold/italics for emphasis. Avoid ALL CAPS or excessive exclamation marks.
- **Lists**: Bulleted lists must follow language-specific punctuation standards.

---

## Linguistic Guidelines

### Writing Style
- **Voice**: Use active voice. Use infinitive for user-driven actions; use imperative when encouraging actions.
- **Inclusivity**: Use neutral, precise terms. Avoid stereotypes (gender, age, ability, background).
- **Capitalization**: Use sentence case (only first word and proper nouns). Do not capitalize months/days unless at sentence start.
- **Anglicisms**: Use only if widely accepted and expected. Avoid unnecessary English terms.
- **Abbreviations**: Use only if widely understood. Write full term + (abbrev) on first mention.

### Terminology & Brand
- **Skyscanner**: Never translate the brand name.
- **"Best"**: Cannot claim "best prices/deals" objectively. Use only if a disclaimer explains the metric (e.g., sorted results). Otherwise, use subjective terms like "epic prices".
- **"Discount"**: Skyscanner never offers discounts. Use only when referring to partner-specific offers. Never use for "price drops" or "deals" (these are market fluctuations).
- **Airport Names**: Do not add the word "aeropuerto" unless required by syntax (e.g., placeholders often handle the specific name). Check official sources for translations.

## Content-Specific Rules

| Content Type | Style Requirements |
| :--- | :--- |
| **UI** | Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. |
| **Marketing** | Creative, fluid, inspirational. Focus on traveler benefits (saving time/money). |
| **SEO** | Natural language (no keyword stuffing). Clear, descriptive titles. |
| **Google Ads** | Extremely concise. Benefits + CTA. No clickbait/unprovable superlatives. |
| **App Store** | Simple, natural phrases. Focus on search/compare/track. |
| **Editorial** | Warm, descriptive, factual. Balance inspiration with concrete tips. |
| **Emails** | Short subject lines. Clear "next steps" (search/manage/book). |
| **Legal** | Precise, neutral, consistent. **No** jokes or emotional language. |

## Quick Reference: Do vs. Don't

- **Buttons**:
    - Do: `Ver ofertas`
    - Don't: `Mostrar una selección de opciones de vuelos disponibles`
- **Error Messages**:
    - Do: `Ha habido un error. Inténtalo de nuevo.`
    - Don't: `Vaya, parece que algo no ha funcionado por alguna misteriosa razón…`
- **Dates**:
    - Do: `18 de marzo de 2020`
    - Don't: `18_de_marzo_de _2020`
- **Sustainability (Claims)**:
    - Do: `Este vuelo emite un 20 % menos de carbono que los vuelos habituales.`
    - Don't: `Este es el vuelo más sostenible/verde.`
- **Capitalization**:
    - Do: `Explora cualquier lugar en mayo.`
    - Don't: `Explora cualquier lugar en Mayo.`
- **Punctuation**:
    - Do: `Haz clic en el botón «Volver a los resultados»`
    - Don't: `Haz clic en el botón VOLVER A LOS RESULTADOS`
- **Consistency**:
    - Do: Use one term consistently (e.g., "Alertas de precios"). Do not switch between synonyms (e.g., "Monitorización de precios").
