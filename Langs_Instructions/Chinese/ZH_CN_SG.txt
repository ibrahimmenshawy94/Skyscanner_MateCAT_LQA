# Chinese Simplified Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
**Tone of Voice**: Skyscanner is a "Geeky Travel Pal."
- **Helpful (Primary)**: Always prioritize clarity, accuracy, and simplicity.
- **Playful**: Use a light, natural energy. Avoid stiff, corporate, or "fake-smart" language. Avoid "LOL-funny" or overly corny humor.
- **Geeky**: Show travel expertise with enthusiasm, but keep it controlled (knowledgeable friend, not a lecture).
- **Rule of Priority**: If in conflict, **Helpful > Playful/Geeky**.

**Mandatory Restrictions**:
- **NO "Discount"**: Skyscanner never offers discounts. Use terms like "deal" or "price drop."
- **NO "Best"**: Do not claim "best" or other superlatives (e.g., "cheapest ever") unless substantiating via a disclaimer. Use "epic prices" or "amazing deals" instead.
- **Sustainability**: Avoid vague terms like "green," "eco," "clean," or "sustainable." Use data-backed comparatives (e.g., "X emits 20% less carbon than Y").
- **No Over-Translation**: Do not translate "Skyscanner."

## Language-Specific Conventions (Chinese Simplified)

### Punctuation & Spacing
- **No extra space**: Do not add spaces between Chinese characters and Arabic numbers, or Chinese characters and Latin characters/alphabets.
- **Commas**: Use `，`.
- **Ellipsis**: Use `……`.
- **Quotation Marks**: Use `「」` for outer, `『』` for inner.
- **Dashes**: Use `——` sparingly; use commas or colons as alternatives to save space.
- **Numbers/Dates/Time**: Always use Arabic numbers. Refrain from using Chinese characters for numbers in dates/time.

### Formatting & Emphasis
- **Emphasis**: Prefer **bold** or *italics*. Do not use ALL CAPS.
- **Exclamation Marks**: Avoid excessive use; never use multiple in a row (e.g., "!!!" is forbidden).
- **Abbreviations/Acronyms**: Use only if widely understood by travelers. If unclear, write out the full term on first mention with the abbreviation in brackets.

## Technical Requirements

### URLs
- **Format**: All lowercase, no spaces (use hyphens `-`), no special/extended characters (use Latin characters only).

### Placeholders & Variables
- **Rules**: Never translate, delete, or change placeholders/variables (e.g., `@@name@@`).
- **Placement**: Keep exact order. If a placeholder is not supported by the tool, keep the English string exactly as is.

### Airport Names & Geo-Terms
- **Transliteration**: Follow official standards/common usage. Be consistent. Do not invent spellings.
- **Search Order**: 1. Official website/travel platforms. 2. Google Maps/Wikipedia. 3. Source language (only if no translation found).
- **"Airport"**: Never add the word "airport" when translating; it is added dynamically in the UI.

## Cultural & Inclusive Language

### Inclusivity
- Avoid gendered language, stereotypes, or assumptions about appearance, family, age, or ability.
- Use neutral, precise terms over labels.

### Emojis & Symbols
- Use sparingly. Never replace key words with emojis.
- **Prohibited**: Avoid culturally inappropriate symbols (e.g., candle/candlelight = "R.I.P." in Chinese culture).
- **Flagging**: Flag culturally inappropriate imagery (e.g., "slanted eyes," offensive clothing/gestures) to Skyscanner.

## Content-Specific Guidelines

### User Interface (UI)
- **Focus**: Short, clear, action-focused.
- **Consistency**: Use identical labels for the same feature throughout the product.
- **Active Voice**: Use imperative/direct verbs. Avoid passive voice.

### Marketing
- **Goal**: Sound natural, fluid, and inspirational.
- **Approach**: Focus on traveler benefits (saving time/money, discovering places). Ensure promises are realistic/compliant.

### SEO & Google Ads
- **SEO**: Natural language; no keyword stuffing. Clear, descriptive headings.
- **Ads**: Highly concise. Use clear benefits and direct CTAs. No clickbait or unprovable claims.

### Legal, Policies, Terms
- **Tone**: Neutral, precise, factual.
- **Constraint**: Do not add interpretations or promises not found in the source text. No emotional language.

### Editorial & Blog
- **Tone**: Warmer and more descriptive than UI, but keep content practical and factual.

### Sustainability
- **Approach**: Use transparent, factual language.
- **Constraint**: Base claims on measurable data. Avoid vague terms ("natural," "green," "non-toxic," "environmentally friendly") unless verifiable. Prefer comparatives ("more sustainable than X").

## Do's and Don'ts (Examples)

| Category | Do | Don't |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tone/Voice** | "Need to know the cheapest day to fly? We got you." | "If you're trying to figure out... [overly verbose/emotional]" |
| **Punctuation** | "Secure the car you really want 🚘" | "Secure the car you REALLY want!!!!! 🚘🚘" |
| **Clarity** | "Compare flights to Tokyo and save time." | "Get the absolute best flights to Tokyo here!" |
| **Accuracy** | "The terms apply to all searches." | "The terms usually apply to most searches." |
| **Clarity** | "Our Drops feature shows flights dropped by 20%." | "Our Drops feature shows flights with a discount of 20%." |
| **Airport Names**| "Paris Charles de Gaulle" | "Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport" |
| **Consistency** | "Book now" | "Ready to start booking?" |
| **Formatting** | "city-break" | "city break", "citybreak" (follow internal standards) |
| **Sustainability**| "Emits 20% less carbon than a typical flight." | "This is a sustainable flight" or "greenest option" |

## Quality & Review Criteria
- **Prioritize**: Clarity > Creativity.
- **Literal vs. Natural**: Prioritize meaning over matching English structure. If a device (pun, rhyme) doesn't translate well, omit or replace it.
- **Verification**: Always search official sources for transit/city names before assuming.
- **Responsibility**: When delivering bad news or apologies, drop the playfulness, be sincere, and focus on the solution.
