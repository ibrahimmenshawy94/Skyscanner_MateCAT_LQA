# sv-SE Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
**Tone Hierarchy**: When prioritizing, **Helpful** > **Geeky** > **Playful**. Always choose clarity over wordplay.
**No "Discounts"**: Skyscanner never offers discounts. Use "You are offered a discount" or similar neutral phrasing. Never imply Skyscanner offers the discount (it is always from partners). Distinguish "deals" and "price drops" from discounts.
**Sustainability**: **Avoid greenwashing**. Use comparatives (e.g., "less CO2 than"), not absolutes (e.g., "green", "eco-friendly", "clean"). Use verifiable data.
**Placeholders**: **Never translate, delete, or modify placeholders** (e.g., `@@city@@`). Keep exact order and spacing. If unsupported, treat as non-translatable text.
**URLs**: **No spaces**, **all lowercase**, **no special characters**. Replace with basic Latin. Greek: Transliterate.

## Tone of Voice: "Geeky Travel Pal"
Our voice must be playful, geeky, and helpful.

- **Playful**: Use a conversational, human tone. Prefer natural rhythm. 
  - **Allowed**: Rhymes, alliteration, metaphors, vivid adjectives (if natural).
  - **Avoid**: Corporate/stiff phrases, forced "LOL-funny" humor, over-the-top puns, elongated vowels, or robotic/flat text.
  - **Rule**: If a device sounds unnatural, skip it.
- **Geeky**: Show excitement for data/patterns. Explain complexity simply as a knowledgeable friend. Avoid lecturing.
- **Helpful**: Foundation of all copy. Be honest and transparent.
  - **Apologies**: Drop playfulness. Apologize once, sincerely. Focus on solutions, not feelings. Avoid cold, robotic apology phrases.

## Technical & Markup Guidelines
- **URLs**: Use hyphens for spaces. All lowercase. No special characters.
- **Placeholders**: Keep syntax identical to source. Handle possessives by restructuring the sentence (e.g., "Barbados' beaches" -> "stränderna på Barbados").
- **Inclusive Language**: Prefer neutral terms. Avoid stereotypes (age, gender, ability, background). Use "hen" if necessary for flow/inclusivity.

## Swedish Linguistic Rules
- **Punctuation**: Use "tydlighetskommatering" (commas to facilitate reading/pauses). 
- **Ellipsis**: **Add a non-breaking space before the ellipsis** (` …`).
- **Exclamation Marks**: Use sparingly. Only if on-brand.
- **Quotation Marks**: Use Swedish style (`” ”` or `' '`). Be careful with straight English quotes (`" "`).
- **Hyphens/Dashes**: 
  - Hyphen (`-`): Compounds and prefixes.
  - Dash (`–`): Intervals (9–17), relationships (stad–landsbygd), pauses, or non-verbatim quotes.
- **Full Stops**: End sentences, abbreviations (`t.ex.`), and times (`13.20`).
- **Capitalization**: 
  - **No Title Case** (except Marketing/Ads). 
  - Months/days: Lowercase unless at start of sentence. 
  - Colon: Capital letter ONLY if following text is a complete sentence.
  - Feature names: Consistent sentence case.
- **Anglicisms**: Avoid unless widely expected by travellers.
- **Abbreviations**: Use only if widely understood. If unsure, spell out first, then add abbreviation in brackets.
- **Formality**: Use **"du"** (familiar). No T/V switching.
- **Active Voice**: **Use imperative for CTAs**. Focus on what the user can do next.
- **Geo-terms/Airport Names**: 
  - Use official local names. 
  - **Never add "airport"** (it is in the placeholder). 
  - Translate "International" to "internationella".

## Content-Specific Guidelines

### UI
- Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. Consistent terminology (e.g., always "Prisbevakningar").

### Marketing
- Natural, fluid, inspirational. Avoid literal translations. Focus on benefits (saving time/money/effort).

### SEO
- Natural language. No keyword stuffing. Clear, descriptive headings/titles.

### Google Ads
- Concise. Clear benefits. Direct CTA. No clickbait or unprovable claims (e.g., "cheapest ever").

### App Store
- Highlight core features. Simple phrases. No superlatives or guarantees.

### Editorial & Blog
- Warm, descriptive, factual. Balance inspiration with practical tips.

### Emails & Push
- Short, clear subject lines. Body copy must be actionable.

### Legal & Policies
- **Precision > Tone**. Neutral, accurate, no emotion/idioms. Do not add interpretations.

## Brand & Feature Usage
- **Skyscanner Brand**: Do not translate. Do not add articles.
- **"Best"**: **Avoid superlatives**. Only use "Best" if there is a clear disclaimer explaining the measurement. Otherwise, use subjective terms like "epic prices".
- **Feature Naming**: Focus on user clarity and meaning. Not literal translation. Ensure it sounds natural for the Swedish market.
- **Emojis**: Use sparingly. Do not add if absent in source. Never use in legal/serious contexts.

## Writing Best Practices
- **Do**: "Äntligen ett sätt att jämföra flyg utan att ha så många flikar öppna."
- **Don't**: "Varning: Vårt flygjämförelseverktyg hittar erbjudanden så snabbt..." (Avoid "fake-smart" corporate speak).
- **Do**: "Behöver du hjälp? Hör av dig till vår kundsupport"
- **Don't**: "Kontakta vår kundsupport om du behöver hjälp med något" (Avoid passive/wordy phrases).
- **Do**: "Boka din drömsemester nu"
- **Don't**: "Boka er drömsemester nu" (Use "du" form).
- **Sustainability**: "Detta flyg släpper ut 20 % mindre koldioxid..." (Good) vs "Detta är ett grönt val" (Bad/Greenwashing).
