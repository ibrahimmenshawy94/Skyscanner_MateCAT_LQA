# Korean Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements

**Priorities**: Always prioritize **Helpful** > **Geeky** > **Playful**. If tone conflicts, be helpful first.
**Brand Identity**: Never translate or adapt the name **Skyscanner**. Do not translate "sky" or "scanner".
**Superlatives & Legal**: **Never** claim "best prices" or "cheapest" as an objective fact. Use subjective claims (e.g., "epic prices") or disclaimers.
**Discounts**: **Never** state "we offer" discounts; Skyscanner does not offer discounts (partners do). Use neutral phrasing: "You are offered a discount."
**Sustainability**: **Avoid greenwashing**. Use comparatives ("X is more sustainable than Y") rather than absolutes ("eco-friendly," "green"). No vague terms.
**Placeholders**: **Never translate, delete, or change placeholders/tags**. Keep exact order. If Korean grammar requires a particle after a placeholder, add a dummy word to ensure natural flow without breaking the tag.

---

## Tone of Voice: "Geeky Travel Pal"

*   **Playful**: Conversational, lively rhythm. Use travel hacks. **Avoid**: Corporate/stiff speak, overly forced "LOL-funny" humor.
*   **Geeky**: Passionate about data, details, and patterns. Keep it controlled; knowledgeable friend, not a lecture.
*   **Helpful**: Make complex info simple. Focus on traveler benefits.
    *   **Bad news**: Drop playfulness, apologize sincerely once, focus on solutions. Avoid cold phrases like "We apologize for the inconvenience."
    *   **Structure**: "Pose question → offer solution."

## Technical Specifications & Formatting

### URL Requirements
*   **Format**: All lowercase, Latin characters only.
*   **Separators**: No spaces; use hyphens (-) only.
*   **No special characters**: Remove or replace with basic Latin letters.

### Punctuation & Spacing (Korean)
*   **Parentheses**: **No spaces** before or after.
*   **Quotation Marks**: Use single quotes (‘ ’). **No space** between quotes and enclosed text. Use double quotes only for direct conversation.
*   **Full Stops**: **No full stops** at the end of titles or headlines.
*   **Ellipses**: Use sparingly. Avoid for emotional emphasis.
*   **Exclamation Marks**: Use sparingly. **Avoid** in formal, error, or customer support contexts. Never use multiple (!!).
*   **Dashes**: Avoid. Restructure sentences to eliminate them.
*   **Hyphens**: Use for standard compound nouns/loanwords only. **Use tildes (~) for ranges** instead of hyphens.
*   **Emphasis**: Use **bold** or single quotes. **Avoid** ALL CAPS (feels like shouting).

### Capitalization
*   **Product Names**: Follow consistent, fixed patterns.
*   **Descriptive terms**: Sentence case.
*   **Retained English terms**: Follow source capitalization.

### Inclusive Language
*   **Avoid**: Stereotypes, gendered assumptions, family/age/ability assumptions.
*   **Preference**: Neutral, precise terms over labels.

## Linguistic Guidelines

### Anglicisms & Abbreviations
*   **Anglicisms**: Use only if widely accepted/expected.
*   **Abbreviations**: Use only if standard (e.g., airport codes). Write full term on first mention if unclear.

### Addressing Users (Formality/Politeness)
*   **T/V Policy**: Maintain a friendly, respectful tone. Avoid distant/stiff formality.
*   **Subjects/Objects**: Korean often omits these; omit if clear in context.
*   **Verb Honorifics**: Rely on verb endings to convey formality rather than forcing "you" (당신).

### CTAs & Active Voice
*   **Active Voice**: Mandatory for headlines and CTAs.
*   **Style**: Short, direct, action-oriented. Focus on what the user can do next.

### Transliteration & Geo-terms
*   **Consistency**: Standardize transliteration. Use established local exonyms (e.g., official city/country names).
*   **Airport Names**: **Never add the word "airport"** to the name; add it as a separate term only if the specific copy requires it.

---

## Content-Specific Rules

### User Interface (UI)
*   **Rule**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string. Consistent terminology (e.g., "Price Alerts" is always "Price Alerts").

### Marketing
*   **Rule**: Natural, fluid, inspirational. Do not mirror English structure too closely. Focus on benefits (time, money, discovery).

### SEO
*   **Rule**: Prioritize natural language. **No keyword stuffing**. Clear, descriptive headings.

### Google Ads
*   **Rule**: Very concise. Strict character limits. No clickbait. No superlatives (no "#1", "best").

### App Store
*   **Rule**: Highlight search, compare, track benefits. Simple, natural phrases.

### Editorial & Blog
*   **Rule**: Warmer, descriptive, but factual. Balance inspiration with concrete tips.

### Legal, Policies, Terms
*   **Rule**: Accuracy, precision, neutrality. **No jokes, emotions, or idioms**. Do not add unauthorized promises.

---

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Emojis & Symbols
*   **Usage**: Sparse. Use only for clarity or light brand touch.
*   **Restrictions**: **Never** in legal/policy/serious incident messages.
*   **Safety**: Avoid laughter emojis (😂, 🤣). If emojis are culturally inappropriate in source, suggest alternatives to Skyscanner.

### Imagery
*   **Flagging**: Report any references (clothing, gestures, food, roles) that are culturally inappropriate in Korea.

---

## Brand Guidelines (Skyscanner)
*   **Naming**: Always use "Skyscanner". Never translate.
*   **Feature Names**: Focus on meaning/clarity. **Avoid literal translations**. If an English term is standard in the local market, keep it. 
*   **"Best" Usage**: Use only with a clear disclaimer explaining the metric. Otherwise, avoid.
