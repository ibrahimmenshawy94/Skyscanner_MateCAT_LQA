# NL Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
**Tone Hierarchy**: When prioritizing tones, always adhere to this order: **1. Helpful, 2. Geeky, 3. Playful.**

**Brand Name**: **Skyscanner** must never be translated. Do not add articles unless required for grammatical correctness.

**"Best" Claims**: Never claim "best prices" or "best deals" as an absolute. Use "Best" only when a clear disclaimer explaining the metric is present. Use subjective claims (e.g., "epic prices") instead.

**Discounts**: **Skyscanner never offers discounts.** Do not imply "we offer" a discount. Use neutral language like "You are offered a discount" only if accurate. Never translate "deal" or "price drop" as a "discount" (legally incorrect).

**Sustainability**: Use comparative language only (e.g., "X is more sustainable than Y"). Never use absolutes (e.g., "eco-friendly," "green," "sustainable"). Avoid "green" imagery.

## Tone of Voice
- **Helpful**: Foundation of all content. Simplify complex travel data. Focus on user needs. Start with benefits/instructions. Apologize only for errors (sincerely, once, focus on solutions).
- **Geeky**: Enthusiastic about data/patterns. Explain complex info simply. Avoid lectures.
- **Playful**: Light, natural energy. Use conversational language. 
    - *Tools*: Rhyme, alliteration, metaphors, vivid adjectives.
    - *Constraint*: If a device is forced, corny, or reduces clarity, **do not use it.** Clarity > Wordplay.
    - *Avoid*: Corporate jargon, "LOL-funny" attempts, stiff phrases.

## Technical & URL Guidelines
- **URLs**:
    - **No spaces**: Use hyphens (-).
    - **Case**: Always lowercase.
    - **Characters**: Latin only. No special characters or accents.
    - **Greek**: Transliterate.
- **Placeholders/Variables**: **Never translate, delete, or change** tags (e.g., `@@city@@`). Maintain exact order. If space is needed, add it manually around the tag. If a tag is uneditable, keep as English.
- **Accessibility**: Follow localized Accessibility guidelines provided separately.

## Language-Specific (Dutch) Rules
- **Punctuation**:
    - **Commas**: Use for clear pauses. No Oxford comma (unless needed for readability).
    - **Ellipsis**: Space before and after, unless breaking a word. No full stop after ellipsis at sentence end.
    - **Exclamation Marks**: Use very sparingly. Only for genuine excitement. Avoid "shouty" tone.
    - **Quotes**: Double straight quotes for dialogue. Single straight quotes for UI elements and thoughts.
    - **Dashes**: **No em-dash.** Use en-dash only for side notes (commas/brackets preferred).
    - **Full Stops**: None in titles, headers, or buttons.
- **Emphasis**: Use *accent aigu* (é) for emphasis. No *accent grave* (è) for emphasis (pronunciation only). Avoid ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation.
- **Capitalization**:
    - **Sentence Case**: Use for titles and CTAs. Only first word and proper nouns capitalized.
    - **Months/Days**: Lowercase in running text.
    - **Colons**: Lowercase after colon, unless: Proper noun follows, list of full sentences follows, quote follows, or text follows "Tip"/"Let op".
- **Spelling/Grammar**:
    - Use official dictionaries (Van Dale, Woordenlijst.org).
    - No unnecessary Anglicisms.
    - No abbreviations unless widely understood (e.g., airport codes).
- **Addressing Users**: Use informal "je/jij" consistently. No T/V switching.

## Formatting & UI
- **Active Voice**: Always use active voice.
- **CTAs**: Use infinitive form (e.g., "Nu boeken"). Keep short and direct.
- **Geographical Terms**: Use official Dutch names. No "luchthaven" prefix inside airport placeholders.
- **Consistency**: Use the same term for features/UI across the product (e.g., always "Prijsalarm").

## Content Type Guidelines
- **UI**: Short, action-focused. One idea per string.
- **Marketing**: Natural, inspirational. Avoid literal translations. Focus on benefits (time/money/discovery).
- **SEO**: Natural language (no keyword stuffing). Descriptive headings.
- **Google Ads**: Very concise. Avoid clickbait/unsubstantiated claims (e.g., "nummer 1").
- **App Store**: Highlight features (search, compare, track).
- **Editorial/Blog**: Warmer, factual, practical.
- **Legal/Policies**: Precise, neutral. No idioms or emotions.

## Common Error Patterns (Do vs. Don't)

### Tone & Style
- **Do**: "Eindelijk een manier om vluchten te vergelijken zonder twintig tabbladen te openen."
- **Don't**: "Waarschuwing: onze vergelijkingstool is zo snel dat het lijkt of je browser een jetpack heeft! Riemen om!" (Too cheesy).

- **Do**: "We kennen het maximale gewicht voor 137 luchtvaartmaatschappijen..."
- **Don't**: "Wij houden het TOT. OP. DE. GRAM. bij..." (Forced tone).

### Formatting
- **Do**: "Reserveer de auto die je écht wilt 🚘"
- **Don't**: "Reserveer de auto die je ECHT wilt" (Avoid ALL CAPS).

- **Do**: "Klik op de knop ‘Terug naar resultaten’..." (Single quotes for UI).
- **Don't**: "Klik op de knop “Terug naar resultaten”..." (Use single).

- **Do**: "Nu boeken"
- **Don't**: "Boek Nu" (Use active infinitive).

### Content Strategy
- **Do**: "Bereik je bestemming binnen je budget."
- **Don't**: "Bereik uw bestemming binnen uw budget." (Use "je/jij" for Dutch).

- **Do**: "Parijs-Charles de Gaulle"
- **Don't**: "Luchthaven Parijs-Charles de Gaulle" (No duplication of "airport").

- **Do**: "Deze vlucht stoot 20% minder koolstof uit..."
- **Don't**: "Dit is de duurzaamste vlucht" (Avoid absolutes/greenwashing).

- **Do**: "Vluchten vergelijken en hotels boeken"
- **Don't**: "Vluchten Vergelijken En Hotels Boeken" (No Title Case).

## Cultural & Inclusive Language
- **Inclusivity**: Avoid stereotypes (nationality, gender, age, family status). Use neutral terms.
- **Emojis**: Use sparingly. Avoid suggestive meanings (aubergine, peach, cherries, banana).
- **Imagery**: Be modest and real. Avoid exaggeration. Flag imagery that might be culturally insensitive.

## Sustainability Definitions
- **Avoid**: "Eco", "Green", "Sustainable", "Clean", "Planet-friendly", "Guilt-free".
- **Use**: Comparative data (e.g., "20% lower carbon emissions").
