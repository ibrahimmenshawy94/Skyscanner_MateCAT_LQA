# HR Style Guide for Skyscanner

## Critical Requirements
*   **Tone Priority**: **Helpful > Geeky > Playful**. Always prioritize clarity and helpfulness.
*   **T/V Policy**: 
    *   **Vi (2nd person plural)**: Used when Skyscanner addresses/encourages/instructs the user.
    *   **Ti (2nd person singular)**: Used when the user addresses/instructs the system.
*   **Grammar**: Follow *Hrvatski pravopis* for standard orthography.
*   **Variables**: Never translate or alter tags/placeholders (e.g., `{{|number|}}`). If grammar requires, create workarounds (e.g., rephrasing) to ensure natural flow.
*   **No Untranslated Content**: Do not leave English text unless specified (e.g., brand names, unsupported tags).

---

## Tone of Voice: Geeky Travel Pal
*   **Playful**: Conversational, lively rhythm, "travel hacks." Avoid corporate jargon or trying too hard to be funny.
*   **Geeky**: Enthusiastic but controlled. Love data/details; explain simply. 
*   **Helpful**: Foundation. Put the traveler first. Start with benefit/instruction. Be honest/transparent, especially with bad news/apologies.
*   **Localisation**: Prioritize meaning and clarity over literal translation. Replace English wordplay (puns/rhymes) with local equivalents or remove if they don't fit naturally.

---

## Technical & URL Guidelines
*   **URLs**: No spaces (use hyphens `-`), all lowercase, no special characters (use Latin equivalents). Only Latin characters allowed.
*   **Placeholders/Tags**: Keep exact order. Add spaces before/after tags to avoid concatenation. 
    *   *Example*: "Search flights to @@city@@" → "Pretražite letove za odredište @@city@@" (adding "odredište" for grammar).
*   **Plurals in UI**:
    *   Single variant: Use parentheses (e.g., `{{|number|}} hotel(a)`).
    *   Multiple variants: Rephrase (e.g., `Više ponuda ({{number}}) od`).

---

## Punctuation & Formatting
*   **Commas/Ellipsis/Full Stops**: Follow *Hrvatski pravopis*.
*   **Ellipsis**: Use `…` (Alt+0133). Not three dots.
*   **Quotation Marks**: UI strings use `„` (Alt+0132) and `”` (Alt+0148).
*   **Dashes**:
    *   En-dash (`–`) for ranges/lists, with non-breaking spaces. Use Alt+0150.
    *   **No Em-dashes (`—`)**.
    *   Do not use dashes to mark pauses. Rephrase instead.
*   **Ampersand**: Always translate `&` to `i`.
*   **Exclamation Marks**: Use sparingly. Avoid "shouting" or spammy marketing.

---

## Terminology & Translation
*   **"To Book"**:
    *   Flights/trains: *kupiti kartu*
    *   Accommodation: *rezervirati smještaj*
    *   Car rental: *unajmiti automobil*
*   **Anglicisms**: Avoid unless widely understood (e.g., brand names).
*   **Abbreviations/Acronyms**: Use only if standard. Explain full term on first mention if risky. Never create new ones to save space.
*   **Brand**: "Skyscanner" never translated. Do not add articles. Never translate "sky" or "scanner".
*   **"Best"**: Only use when clear disclaimer explains how measured. Otherwise, use subjective terms like "epic" or "amazing."
*   **Discounts**: Never imply "we offer" a discount. Use "You are offered a discount" or similar neutral phrasing. Do not confuse "deal"/"price drop" with "discount."

---

## Numbers, Dates & Units
*   **Dates**: Day (lowercase month) Year (e.g., *12. travnja 2019.*).
*   **Day/Month Abbreviations**: pon, uto, sri, čet, pet, sub, ned | sij, velj, ožu, tra, svi, lip, srp, kol, ruj, lis, stu, pro.
*   **Time**: 24-hour format (e.g., 9:00).
*   **Numbers**: Comma for decimal, point for thousand (e.g., 1.000.000,00). 1–10 can be alphabetic if preferred for flow.
*   **Units**: Always localize. Imperial -> include metric in brackets (e.g., 5 in/13 cm). Use non-breaking space before unit/%.

---

## Capitalization
*   **Style**: Sentence case (first word + proper nouns).
*   **Colons**: Do not capitalize after colon (unless proper noun follows).
*   **Titles/CTAs**: Avoid title case.

---

## Content Types
*   **UI**: Short, clear, action-focused. One idea per string.
*   **Marketing**: Fluid, creative, inspirational, benefit-focused.
*   **SEO**: Natural language, no keyword stuffing.
*   **Legal**: Precise, neutral. No jokes/idioms.
*   **Sustainability**: 
    *   **Strict No-Go**: Never claim "sustainable", "eco", "green", "clean", "planet-friendly", "guilt-free". 
    *   **Instruction**: Use comparatives ("X emits less carbon than Y"). No superlatives ("the greenest"). Use factual/transparent data.

---

## Cultural Sensitivity & Imagery
*   **Emojis**: Use sparingly and only if in source. Never replace key words with emojis.
*   **Imagery/Flags**: Flag references that could be culturally inappropriate (gestures, clothing, roles).
*   **Transliteration/Geo-terms**: Use official/widely used local names. Do not translate airport names (e.g., keep "Charles de Gaulle"). Never add "airport" if not in the name (it populates via placeholder).

---

## Quick Reference: Do & Don't
*   **Do**: Use active voice. Use "Vi" for system-to-user instructions. Translate "City break" to "odmor u gradu".
*   **Don't**: Don't use "city-break". Don't use Em-dashes. Don't use "best" as an unverified superlative. Don't mix English/local words to appear "trendy." Don't use all caps for emphasis.
